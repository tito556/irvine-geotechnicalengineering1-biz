In Irvine, geophysical investigations adapt to the complex alluvial and sedimentary geology of Orange County, shaped by the Santa Ana River and nearby fault systems like the Newport-Inglewood zone. Our approach addresses site-specific challenges—from assessing basin-edge effects to complying with CBC/ASCE 7-22 requirements for seismic site class. We routinely deploy MASW / VS30 profiling to determine shear wave velocity to 30 meters depth, a critical parameter for structural design. For sites with limited access or ambient noise constraints, the HVSR microtremor survey (Nakamura method) provides a rapid, non-invasive alternative to estimate fundamental site period and layering.

These methods support a broad range of projects, from mid-rise commercial developments in the Irvine Business Complex to infrastructure and public-school modernizations requiring precise seismic hazard evaluation. Where stratigraphic detail or depth-to-bedrock is needed, seismic tomography complements velocity profiles by imaging subsurface geometry. The resulting data directly informs foundation design, liquefaction assessments, and ground-improvement strategies across the city’s engineered fill and older alluvial terraces.

Geophysics in Irvine provides a non-invasive lens into the subsurface, essential for navigating the complex geological conditions of Southern California. Our geophysical services cover seismic refraction, electrical resistivity, ground-penetrating radar (GPR), and downhole logging, all calibrated to local stratigraphy dominated by alluvial deposits, expansive clay layers, and the proximity of the San Joaquin Hills thrust fault system. These investigations are critical for complying with California Building Code Chapter 18 and local City of Irvine grading ordinances, which often mandate detailed subsurface characterization to mitigate risks from liquefaction and differential settlement. A thorough geophysical survey often begins with a targeted geotechnical investigation to correlate geophysical anomalies with physical soil properties, ensuring data interpretation is grounded in direct measurements.

Our field methodology strictly adheres to ASTM International standards to deliver defensible data for regulatory approval in Irvine. Seismic surveys, for instance, follow ASTM D5777 for crosshole and downhole testing to derive shear-wave velocity profiles necessary for Site Class determination per ASCE 7 and the CBC. Electrical resistivity imaging is performed in accordance with ASTM D6431, effectively mapping variations in soil moisture and potential groundwater pathways within the ancient San Diego Creek alluvium. When integrated with direct penetration data from cone penetration testing (CPT) or standard penetration testing (SPT), these methods provide a high-resolution geostratigraphic model. For assessing near-surface fill and recompaction requirements, In-Situ methods are deployed to verify that engineered fills meet the 90% relative compaction threshold commonly specified by Irvine project geotechnical engineers, avoiding costly over-excavation.

Typical projects in Irvine demand a fusion of geophysics and traditional geotechnical exploration to address site-specific challenges. For high-density residential and mixed-use developments in areas like the Irvine Business Complex or Great Park Neighborhoods, we utilize flat dilatometer testing (DMT) to assess lateral stress and constrained modulus, parameters vital for designing deep excavations and shoring adjacent to existing infrastructure. Infrastructure improvements, such as bridge widenings on the I-405 or Jamboree Road extensions, require plate load tests (PLT) to confirm the bearing capacity and modulus of subgrade reaction for mechanically stabilized earth walls and approach slabs. Similarly, the Ménard pressuremeter test (PMT) is employed for deep foundation design in the variable Pleistocene-age terrace deposits, providing accurate stress-strain curves that prevent overly conservative pile lengths and reduce concrete volumes.

The process begins with a desktop study of historic aerials and well logs, followed by a field program designed to optimize survey line placement and testing depths. Deliverables include 2D and 3D subsurface models with clearly annotated geophysical profiles correlated to boring logs and CPT soundings, providing a seamless transition from raw data to actionable engineering parameters. The core value proposition is risk reduction: by integrating field density testing with GPR to confirm utility clearances and backfill integrity, we prevent construction delays and ensure compliance with the stringent grading and erosion control standards enforced by the City of Irvine Public Works Department. This multi-method approach delivers a comprehensive ground model, allowing structural engineers to design foundations with confidence and contractors to bid with certainty.
